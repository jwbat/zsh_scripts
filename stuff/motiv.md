## concise, tried-and-true ways to get moving

1. Break the task into the smallest possible next step.  
2. Use the 10-minute rule: do it for just 10 minutes, then reassess.  
3. Make a short list of only 1–3 priorities for today.  
4. Start before you feel ready; action often creates motivation.  
5. Remove friction: clear the desk, close distracting tabs, silence the phone.  
6. Lower the bar on the first draft or first attempt.  
7. Tie the task to a concrete reason that matters to you.  
8. Work in short timed bursts, then take a brief break.  
9. Set a tiny reward for finishing the next step.  
10. Change your environment: different room, café, library, or standing desk.  
11. Tell someone what you plan to do and when.  
12. Track a streak of days you show up, even imperfectly.  
13. Sleep, eat, hydrate, and move a little; low energy often feels like low motivation.  
14. Reduce the scope: do “good enough” instead of “all of it.”  
15. If you’re stuck, write down the exact thing you’re avoiding and why.  
16. Make the task more interesting with music, a timer, or a challenge.  
17. Pair it with something easy, like tea or a favorite playlist.  
18. Ask whether the goal is truly yours or something you feel you “should” do.  
19. Stop for the day if you’re genuinely burned out; rest can restore drive.  
20. If low motivation lasts a long time or comes with hopelessness, get support from a professional.

A practical reset is: choose one task, shrink it to the next physical action, set a 10-minute timer, and begin.
